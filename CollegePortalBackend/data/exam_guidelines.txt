COLLEGE EXAMINATION GUIDELINES
Examination Overview

The college follows a semester-based examination system designed to evaluate students through continuous assessment and end-semester examinations. Students are expected to follow all examination rules and academic regulations issued by the Examination Cell.

The academic calendar published at the beginning of each semester contains the examination schedule, important deadlines, and result declaration dates.

Examination Eligibility

To be eligible for university examinations, students must:

Maintain at least 75% attendance in every subject.
Complete all mandatory practical sessions and laboratory work.
Submit assignments, projects, and internal assessments before the prescribed deadlines.
Clear all pending academic dues and administrative requirements.

Students with attendance below the required percentage may not receive permission to appear for the end-semester examinations unless officially approved by the college authorities.

Examination Pattern

The evaluation consists of two components:

Internal Assessment – 30 Marks

Internal assessment may include:

Class Tests
Assignments
Presentations
Laboratory Performance
Projects
Class Participation
Attendance

Faculty members evaluate students continuously throughout the semester.

End Semester Examination – 70 Marks

The university conducts the final examination at the end of every semester.

The examination generally consists of:

Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
Short Answer Questions
Long Descriptive Questions
Numerical Problems (where applicable)

The duration of the examination is generally 3 hours.

Passing Criteria

Students must obtain:

Minimum 40% overall score.
Satisfy the minimum passing criteria prescribed by the university for both theory and practical examinations.
Complete all compulsory practical examinations where applicable.

Students failing to achieve the required marks must reappear according to university regulations.

Hall Ticket Guidelines

Every student must carry:

Printed Hall Ticket
Valid College Identity Card

Students without a valid hall ticket may not be permitted to enter the examination hall.

Students should verify that their:

Name
Roll Number
Examination Centre
Subject Codes

are correctly printed on the hall ticket before the examination.

Examination Rules

Students must:

Reach the examination hall at least 30 minutes before the scheduled time.
Occupy only their allotted seat.
Carry only permitted stationery items.
Follow all instructions given by invigilators.
Maintain silence inside the examination hall.

Students are advised to carefully read the question paper before starting the examination.

Prohibited Items

The following items are strictly prohibited inside the examination hall:

Mobile Phones
Smart Watches
Bluetooth Devices
Calculators (unless permitted)
Written Notes
Books
Electronic Gadgets

Possession of prohibited items may result in disciplinary action.

Unfair Means Policy

Students caught using unfair means during examinations will face disciplinary proceedings.

Examples include:

Copying from another student
Possession of unauthorized study material
Using electronic devices for cheating
Impersonation
Communicating during the examination

Depending on the severity of the misconduct, penalties may include:

Cancellation of the examination
Suspension from future examinations
Cancellation of the semester
Other disciplinary actions as determined by the university.
Revaluation and Rechecking

Students dissatisfied with their examination results may apply for:

Answer Sheet Revaluation
Answer Sheet Photocopy
Marks Verification

Applications must be submitted within the time period specified by the Examination Cell.

Applicable fees must be paid during the application process.

Supplementary Examinations

Students who fail one or more subjects may appear for supplementary examinations conducted by the university.

Eligibility and examination schedules are announced after the declaration of semester results.

Result Declaration

Semester examination results are published on the official college portal.

Students can view their:

Subject-wise Marks
Grade
SGPA
CGPA
Pass/Fail Status

using their registered student login credentials.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the minimum attendance required to appear for examinations?

Students must maintain at least 75% attendance to be eligible for university examinations.

What is the passing percentage?

Students must secure a minimum 40% overall score to pass the examination.

Can I appear for the examination without a hall ticket?

No. Every student must carry a valid hall ticket and college identity card.

What happens if I fail a subject?

Students may appear for supplementary examinations as per university rules.

Can I apply for revaluation?

Yes. Students can apply for revaluation, photocopy of the answer sheet, or marks verification within the prescribed deadline after results are announced.
