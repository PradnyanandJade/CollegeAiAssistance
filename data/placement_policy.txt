COLLEGE PLACEMENT POLICY
Placement Overview

The Training and Placement Cell (TPC) is responsible for coordinating all campus recruitment activities and preparing students for employment opportunities. The Placement Cell acts as a bridge between students and recruiting organizations by organizing placement drives, internships, career guidance sessions, and industry interactions.

Campus placement opportunities are offered to eligible students during their final year of study.

Placement Eligibility

Students become eligible for campus placements from the final year of their academic program.

To participate in campus recruitment, students must satisfy the following eligibility criteria:

Minimum CGPA: 6.5
No active backlogs
Completion of all mandatory academic requirements
Registration with the Training and Placement Cell
Submission of an updated resume before the placement season

Companies may specify additional eligibility criteria depending on their recruitment requirements.

Placement Registration Process

Students wishing to participate in campus placements must:

Register with the Training and Placement Cell.
Complete their student profile on the placement portal.
Upload an updated resume.
Verify academic records.
Attend the mandatory pre-placement orientation session.

Only registered students will receive placement notifications.

Placement Process

The standard campus recruitment process consists of the following stages:

Step 1 – Company Registration

Companies register with the Training and Placement Cell and provide:

Job Description
Eligibility Criteria
Salary Package
Selection Process
Interview Schedule

Eligible students may apply for the company through the placement portal.

Step 2 – Aptitude Test

The aptitude assessment may include:

Quantitative Aptitude
Logical Reasoning
Verbal Ability
Analytical Skills

Only shortlisted candidates proceed to the next round.

Step 3 – Technical Interview

Technical interviews evaluate:

Programming Skills
Core Subject Knowledge
Problem Solving Ability
Project Experience
Communication Skills

Students should be prepared to explain academic projects and internships.

Step 4 – HR Interview

The HR interview generally evaluates:

Communication Skills
Personality
Career Goals
Teamwork
Leadership Qualities
Cultural Fit

Final selection is based on overall performance throughout the recruitment process.

Placement Rules

Students are expected to:

Attend every scheduled interview on time.
Maintain professional behavior throughout the placement process.
Carry their college identity card during placement activities.
Follow the instructions issued by the Placement Cell.

Students should inform the Placement Cell immediately if they are unable to attend any scheduled interview.

One Student One Offer Policy

Students selected by one company offering a package above ₹8 LPA are not allowed to participate in additional placement drives organized by the college.

This policy ensures fair placement opportunities for all eligible students.

Exceptions, if any, are decided solely by the Training and Placement Cell.

Internship Opportunities

The Placement Cell also facilitates internship opportunities for eligible students.

Internships may be offered during:

Summer Vacation
Winter Internship Programs
Final Semester Industrial Training

Performance during internships may lead to Pre-Placement Offers (PPOs).

Placement Training

To improve employability, the college organizes regular training programs, including:

Aptitude Training
Technical Skill Development
Coding Practice Sessions
Resume Building Workshops
Mock Interviews
Group Discussion Sessions
Soft Skills Training
Communication Skills Development

Students are encouraged to attend all training sessions before the placement season.

Placement Statistics

The Placement Cell publishes annual placement reports containing:

Number of Participating Companies
Number of Students Placed
Highest Salary Package
Average Salary Package
Department-wise Placement Statistics

These reports are available through the college placement portal.

Code of Conduct

Students participating in campus placements must:

Provide accurate academic information.
Submit only genuine documents.
Avoid accepting multiple offers in violation of placement policies.
Maintain professional communication with recruiters.
Follow the placement schedule issued by the college.

Violation of placement rules may result in suspension from future placement activities.

Frequently Asked Questions
Who is eligible for campus placements?

Students become eligible for campus placements from their final year, provided they meet the required eligibility criteria.

What is the minimum CGPA required?

Students must have a minimum CGPA of 6.5.

Can students with active backlogs participate?

No. Students with active backlogs are not eligible for campus placements unless the recruiting company specifies otherwise.

What is the campus placement process?

The placement process generally includes:

Company Registration
Aptitude Test
Technical Interview
HR Interview
Can I participate in another placement drive after receiving an offer above ₹8 LPA?

No. Students selected by one company offering a package above ₹8 LPA are not allowed to participate in additional campus placement drives.

Does the college provide placement training?

Yes. The Training and Placement Cell conducts aptitude training, technical workshops, coding sessions, resume building workshops, mock interviews, and soft skills development programs.
